Theorems 7.6-7.7 — Robust covariance consistency

Textbook statement

With heteroskedasticity, the population asymptotic covariance of OLS is the sandwich matrix

V_\beta = Q^{-1}\Omega Q^{-1}, \qquad \Omega = \mathbb{E}[e_i^2 X_iX_i^\top].

Theorem 7.6 states that the feasible HC0 estimator converges to this sandwich target:

\hat V_{\beta,n}^{HC0} \xrightarrow{p} V_\beta.

Theorem 7.7 adds the finite-sample variants HC1, HC2, and HC3. HC1 differs by a scalar degrees-of-freedom factor, while HC2 and HC3 require leverage conditions ensuring the additional denominator corrections are asymptotically negligible.

In Lean, the core assumptions are bundled as RobustCovarianceConsistencyConditions μ X e, with the textbook-compatible alias SampleHC0Assumption76 μ X e. The current component-facing theorems also take explicit measurability and bounded-weight remainder controls.

Proof sketch

The robust covariance proof is a sandwich assembly:

  1. Prove the ideal score covariance matrix converges to \Omega.
  2. Show replacing true errors by OLS residuals only adds negligible remainder terms. These are the cross-weight and quadratic-weight controls.
  3. Combine the middle-matrix convergence with \hat Q_n^{-1}\to_p Q^{-1} on both sides.
  4. For HC1, multiply by a scalar factor converging to one.
  5. For HC2 and HC3, add leverage controls to show their extra corrections vanish.

Tactic-by-tactic walkthrough

The component-facing HC0 theorem is a wrapper around the core sandwich theorem: derive measurability from component assumptions, then pass through the remainder controls.

have hScore_meas :=
    sampleScoreCovarianceStar_stack_aestronglyMeasurable_of_components
      (μ := μ) (X := X) (e := e) (y := y) β h.toSampleMomentAssumption71 hmodel
      hX_meas he_measexact olsHeteroskedasticCovarianceStar_tendstoInMeasure_of_bounded_weights
    (μ := μ) (X := X) (e := e) (y := y)
    h β hmodel hScore_meas hCrossWeight hQuadWeight

Downstream uses

These covariance consistency theorems feed the heteroskedastic versions of Chapter 7 inference: standard-normal t statistics, confidence interval coverage, and one-degree-of-freedom Wald statistics.
