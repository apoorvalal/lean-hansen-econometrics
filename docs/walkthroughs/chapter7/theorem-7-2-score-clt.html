Theorem 7.2 — Score CLT

Textbook statement

For an i.i.d. sample with score vector e_i X_i, assume the Chapter 7 score CLT conditions: the score has mean zero, the relevant second moments exist, and the score covariance matrix

\Omega = \mathbb{E}[e_i^2 X_i X_i^\top]

is finite. Then the scaled sample score converges in distribution:

\sqrt{n}\,\hat g_n(e) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}\sum_{i=1}^n e_i X_i \Rightarrow N(0,\Omega).

In Lean, the assumptions are bundled as ScoreCLTConditions μ X e, with the textbook-compatible alias SampleCLTAssumption72 μ X e. The theorem is stated for the stacked sample cross moment sampleCrossMoment (stackRegressors X n ω) (stackErrors e n ω).

Proof sketch

The Lean proof follows the textbook route but makes the Cramer-Wold step explicit.

  1. Fix a direction a \in \mathbb{R}^k.
  2. Apply Mathlib’s real-valued i.i.d. CLT to the scalar sequence (e_i X_i)^\top a.
  3. Rewrite the scalar variance as the quadratic form a^\top \Omega a.
  4. Use the reusable Cramer-Wold bridge to assemble the vector-valued Gaussian convergence.

Downstream uses

The score CLT is the distributional input for Theorem 7.3’s OLS asymptotic normality and for the later t/Wald inference pages. In particular, scoreProjection_olsBetaStar_tendstoInDistribution_gaussian_covariance uses it after applying the inverse population Gram matrix and Slutsky’s theorem.
